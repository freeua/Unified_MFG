    Are any products VAT exempt?

    Yes. UNIFIED MFG doesn't charge VAT on kid's, youth, and baby apparel shipped from our EU facility to the UK. That's because these categories are VAT-exempt by UK law.

    Where is UNIFIED MFG registered as a VAT payer?

    UNIFIED MFG is currently registered as a VAT payer in Latvia, the UK, and Spain.

    That means that we'll always charge a 21% VAT rate on orders going to Latvia and Spain, and 20% on orders going to the UK. That's because those count as domestic orders and can't have a 0% VAT rate.

    How does VAT apply to my warehoused products in Latvia?

    If your products are stored at our Latvia warehouse:

    You may be liable to register as a VAT payer in Latvia, since that's where your products are stored. This is regardless of your sales threshold.

    If you're registered as a VAT payer, charge VAT on all of your orders going to EU member states (including orders with non-warehoused products).

    UNIFIED will add a 21% VAT charge on your monthly storage fees, and on fulfillment and shipping fees for orders going to EU member states. If you provide us with a valid VAT ID, we'll charge 0% VAT on fulfillment fees. We're still obligated to collect 21% on fulfillment, storage, and shipping for orders going to Latvia. And we'll charge 20% on fulfillment and shipping fees to the UK, since we're also registered there.

    How does VAT apply to my warehoused products in North Carolina?

    We don't charge you VAT for orders stored in North Carolina with a shipping destination in the European Union.

    But you may be liable to charge VAT on orders going to EU member states. That means you would register as a VAT payer, charge customers in the EU, and remit VAT to the country in which you're registered.

    Do I have to charge VAT?

    If your business meets the requirements to register as a VAT payer and collect VAT, then yes. You charge VAT from your customers and remit it to the corresponding country in which you're registered.

    Do I need to register as a VAT payer in each country I sell in?

    If your business meets the requirements to register as a VAT payer and collect VAT, then yes. You charge VAT from your customers and remit it to the corresponding country in which you're registered.

    How do I register for a VAT ID?

    To register for a VAT ID, you'll need to provide specific documents to the State Revenue Service of the country in which you're registering. In most cases, you'll be asked to fill in a registration application and provide the following information:

    1. 1. Legal entity information (registration ID, legal name, legal address) and contact info
    2. 2. Personal information (personal ID, date of birth, name and surname, place of residence)
    3. 3. Description of business activity you are performing
    4. 4. Your bank account details
    5. 5. Other questions may apply (eg. business partners' information, computer accounting information, core capital information, etc.)

    How do I configure VAT on my ecommerce platform?

    Each platform deals with VAT differently, so we recommend you use your platform's resources. Here are some links to some of our most popular integrations.

    Shopify Etsy Woocommerce Squarespace Wix
    Weebly Storenvy BigCommerce Ecwid
    Amazon BigCartel Gumroad eBay

    Where can I get help with understanding my VAT obligations?

    We strongly suggest consulting with a tax professional to understand all of your tax obligations – both for VAT and US-based taxes.

    You can also check out services like VATGlobal, FBA-hero, and SimplyVAT for VAT questions and setup help.

    Where can I find my VAT report?

    Monthly VAT reports are used for accounting needs, primarily for those who are registered VAT payers.

    You can find them on your UNIFIED MFG dashboard by going to Billing > Payment History.

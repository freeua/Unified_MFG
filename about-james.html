    ABOUT
    UNIFIED MFG.

    Hi, how are you?

    Thanks for dropping by!

    If you’re curious about what we do and who we really are (we’re blushing), read up our short story. If, after reading our bio, you think we’re okay guys and you want to hireus, work with us or just be our friend, please say hi!

    My name is James Hill.

    If there’s one thing that I can tell you with 100% certainty, it’s that I have been obsessed with music and design for as long as I can remember.

    How it all began

    I blame my old man.

    He played music nonstop all day every day from soulful beats of Bob Marley to the upbeat, never-giving-up-the-music of Bruce Springsteen.

    On weekends, we used to laze around in our living room and listen to his massive vinyl collection. At a young age of eight, I already knew what I wanted to be — a musician!

    As a teen

    I would spend hours with friends pretending we were in a band. I was the lead singer.

    We were destined to make great records and tour the world.

    One fateful day, when we finally decided to practice, my bubble was burst when I found out that I was tone deaf!

    I was literally the worst singer you could possibly imagine.

    I worked every shitty job you could imagine so that I could stay connected to music. Everybody knows that once the music has gotten a hold of you, it's super hard to leave.

    I felt at home with the musicians. I loved how honest I could be and fast wecould connect.

    (To this day, this is still my favorite thing about artists — who they really are. I just want to be around them or talk to them. They inspire me daily.)

    FAST FORWARD TO A FEW YEARS LATER

    I created a company called LiveUnsigned.com in Burbank where we tapped bands to perform live, put their performances online (this is pre-Youtube) and then send the tapes to the record Execs.

    That got me a relationship with Mio Vuckio who kindly allowed me to bring live bands.

    This opportunity later let me to manage producers and songwriters at Interscope/ Geffen/Confidential Records where I got my first platinum record managing the day-to-day of Mix the Formal - on Keyshia Cole’s "The Wayit is.”

    We also worked with Gwen Stefani, Missy Elliott, INXS, Dr Dre, Bone Thugs and hundreds of independent artists and bands.

    During this time, I decided to start a merchandise company to create merch for the touring musicians.

    We started swagging for the likes of Bob Dylan, Smashing Pumpkins, and my little company became bigger and bigger which led to the birth of...
